Key Findings
Options nearly universal in this cohort. 88% of companies use stock options, driven by a peer group built largely from recently-IPO'd or PE-backed consumer/DTC brands whose programs originated in a private-company context. Options remain the single most prevalent vehicle by far.
RSUs broadly adopted but still secondary. 65% use time-based RSUs — most often introduced at or shortly after IPO as a complement to options. The trajectory across filing years clearly trends from options-only toward SO+RSU and eventually RSU-led blends as companies mature post-IPO.
Performance equity nearly absent. Only 1 of 17 companies (HIMS) uses performance-based equity in its core annual program — PRSUs at 35% of annual LTI. No company has implemented traditional financial-metric PSUs in a formal annual cycle. The peer group is overwhelmingly pre-performance-equity.
PSOs not present in any annual program. Zero companies use performance stock options in their recurring grant cycle. This vehicle is entirely absent across the full peer group on a core annual program basis.
Three dominant program archetypes. Programs cluster around: (1) Options-only — 6 companies (35%); (2) SO + RSU blend — 8 companies (47%); (3) RSU-dominant or multi-vehicle — 2 companies (12%). HIMS stands alone as the only company with performance equity in its annual cycle.
Several programs nascent or non-recurring. BRLT, TDUP, LVLU, RVLV, and AKA had not yet established a structured annual LTI cycle at time of filing. Grants primarily reflect new-hire, IPO, or PE-conversion packages rather than repeatable program design.
