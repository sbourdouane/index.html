Key Findings
Options-dominant peer group. 76% of companies used stock options, reflecting a cohort of recently-IPO'd consumer/DTC brands whose programs were built in a private-company or early-stage context. Options remain the most prevalent single vehicle.
RSUs broadly adopted but secondary. 59% use time-based RSUs — often introduced at or shortly after IPO as a complement to options. The trend across filings shows a clear shift from options-only toward SO+RSU or RSU-only programs over time.
Performance equity nearly absent. Only 1 of 17 companies (WRBY) uses performance shares — and those are stock-price-hurdle PSUs embedded in a one-time founders grant, not traditional financial-metric PSUs in a repeating annual program. No company has introduced traditional PSUs as part of its regular annual LTI cycle.
PSOs not used in any regular program. Zero companies use performance stock options in their recurring annual grant cycle. HIMS FY2020 had CEO price-hurdle options, but these were a one-time SPAC-era grant — not an annual program feature.
Program mix typically simple: SO-only or SO+RSU. Most companies fall into one of three archetypes: (1) Options-only — 5 companies (29%); (2) RSU-only — 2 companies (12%); (3) SO+RSU blend — 7 companies (41%). Only one company combines performance equity with time-based vehicles.
Many programs are nascent / non-recurring. Several companies (BRLT, TDUP, LVLU, RVLV, AKA) had not yet established a structured annual LTI cycle at the time of their most recent filing. Equity grants largely reflected hire/IPO packages rather than repeatable program design.
